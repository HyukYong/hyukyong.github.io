Chembook · for students

Chemistry you can
actually see.

Chembook turns the abstract parts of chemistry — orbitals, vibrations, reactions, dynamics — into things you can rotate, run, and play with. Every visual is built on real quantum-chemistry data, not cartoons. An interactive lab that fits in your pocket.

The 3D visualizer

One hub, five ways to look at matter.

The heart of the app — rotate, slide, and simulate molecules in three dimensions, every view rendered from real computed data.

01 / Reactions

91 mechanisms, frame by frame

Step through reactant → transition state → product as real keyframes, each with kinetics, conditions, and stereochemistry notes — plus toggles for atom labels, bonds, and curved arrows.

SN1 / SN2Diels–AlderaldolGrignardWittig
02 / Orbitals

Molecular orbitals as real isosurfaces

Tap any level on the energy ladder to render its orbital from actual computed wavefunctions (RHF/6-31G(d)). Slide the isovalue to grow or shrink the electron cloud, and switch canonical ↔ localized.

RHF / 6-31G(d)isovalue slidercanonical / localized
03 / Structures

Conformers & stereochemistry, annotated

Rotate glucose chairs, ring-flip methylcyclohexane, inspect amino acids, crystal unit cells, and Watson–Crick DNA base pairs — with notes on why each shape is favored.

chairs & ring-flipsamino acidsunit cellsDNA base pairs
04 / Vibrations

Normal modes & symmetry, animated

Watch every normal mode animate with displacement arrows, labeled with frequency, IR/Raman activity, and point-group symmetry — the spectroscopy students most have to imagine, made visible.

displacement arrowsIR / Raman activitypoint groups
05 / Molecular dynamics

A live simulator you can drive

A real-time Lennard-Jones / TIP4P engine — drag temperature and density to melt, freeze, boil, or condense the system, and watch the pair potential and radial distribution function update as the phase changes.

Lennard-Jones / TIP4Pmelt / freeze / boillive RDF

More to explore

A whole chemistry desk in your pocket.

Beyond the visualizer, Chembook bundles the reference, tools, and practice you actually reach for before an exam.

Periodic Table

All 118 elements with interactive trends — electronegativity, ionization energy, atomic radius — plus filters, per-element detail, and side-by-side comparison.

Spectroscopy

¹H and ¹³C NMR with an interactive (n+1) splitting-tree builder and a chemical-shift reference, alongside IR/Raman, mass spec, and UV-Vis.

Encyclopedia

CODATA physical constants you tap to copy at full precision, IUPAC nomenclature, a glossary, and formula sheets — the reference desk that matches the exam.

Calculator

Unit conversion, molar mass, molarity, dilution, pH, gas law, electrode potential, and stoichiometry — all in one place.

Quiz

Five game modes — from dragging elements into the table to naming vibrations by symmetry — to drill what you’ve learned.

Notes

Keep your own notes and sketch structures as you study, right alongside everything else.

Why Chembook

Real data. Built for exams.

01

Real data, not illustrations

Orbitals, vibrations, and reaction geometries come from quantum-chemistry calculations (PySCF) — what you see is physically grounded, the same engine that powers Qemica.

02

Built for exams

Coverage spans AP Chemistry units and the GRE Chemistry subject test — physical, organic, inorganic, and analytical.

03

Interactive first

Rotate, slide, simulate. Most modules respond to touch in real time, so you learn by doing, not memorizing a diagram.

04

Works offline

All the data ships with the app — every orbital and mechanism is there with no connection required.
